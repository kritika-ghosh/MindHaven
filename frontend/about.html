Abstract

Mental burnout is a significant occupational and psychological issue that arises especially among college students who regularly face academic and social pressure in their life. This paper introduces a multimodal system that detects mental burnout in students which integrates data in a flow starting from questionnaire, facial expressions, and voice sentiment analysis to provide a more unbiased result. Due to the lack of a suitable public dataset for such a problem, a new custom dataset has been created with great precautions.

The architecture processes each data path starting with an initial questionnaire which provides a self-reported baseline, facial analysis which computes the eye and mouth aspect ratios (EAR/MAR) and detects emotions using the Deepface library, and voice sentiment analysis via VADER library which calculates the compound sentiment of how the person is feeling. These variety of features are then fused and fed into a CatBoost machine learning model, which achieved an accuracy of 78%.

I. Introduction & Motivation

In today’s society, mental burnout has come out to be a crucial challenge to an individual’s overall well-being and productivity. The pressures of academic stress and changes in the surroundings can make college students highly prone to burnout and thus need detection and support.

Traditional methods have relied majorly on self-report clinical questionnaires, which tend to miss real-time emotional fluctuations and suffer from subjective individual bias. By utilizing multi-modal fusion, our model combines data from three distinct sources (arranged survey, facial aspect ratios, and acoustic sentiment) to create an objective, unbiased evaluation.

II. Architecture & Methodology

Our empirical methodology is divided into a structured three-part pipeline:

  • Psychometric Baseline (Survey): A questionnaire covering aspects of academic burnout derived from the Maslach Burnout Inventory (MBI) to set a self-reported cognitive state.
  • Facial Geometric Indicators (Computer Vision): A 20-second camera capture extracts EAR and MAR landmarks dynamically. Deepface is used to compute facial expression scores (Positive, Neutral, Negative).
  • Acoustic Sentimental Channel: Live vocals are captured and processed through Speech Recognition. Sentiment compound scores are computed via the Valence Aware Dictionary and sEntiment Reasoner (VADER) library.

III. Geometric Feature Formulas

1. Eye Aspect Ratio (EAR)

Calculates eye fatigue based on vertical and horizontal landmark distances:

EAR = (dist(P2, P6) + dist(P3, P5)) / (2 * dist(P1, P4))

2. Mouth Aspect Ratio (MAR)

Measures physical tension and yawning frequencies based on vertical height and width:

MAR = (max_y(INNER_MOUTH) - min_y(INNER_MOUTH)) / dist(L454, L234)

IV. Results & Benchmarks

Through rigorous evaluation, the CatBoost Classifier yielded the highest accuracy of 78% on the custom multimodal dataset, outperforming traditional classifiers such as Random Forest (69%) and Logistic Regression (54%).

V. Conclusion & Future Directions

This project successfully validated a multimodal machine learning framework that evaluates student burnout levels objectively. Feature importance graphs confirm that physiological facial indicators (MAR & EAR) are the most significant markers, proving the necessity of multi-modal features over subjective reports.

Future extensions will focus on gathering long-term temporal sequences from individuals to map fatigue fluctuations throughout semesters and incorporating wearable sensors like heart-rate variability for continuous diagnosis.
